About

I write about literature, history, philosophy, and the classical tradition—subjects that reward slow thinking and repay rereading.

My approach is straightforward: read closely, think independently, and argue when necessary. I'm more interested in what's true than what's fashionable, which occasionally means disagreeing with received wisdom.

I'm drawn to questions that don't have clean answers: What makes a work of literature endure? How do we read ancient texts without flattening them into contemporary categories? Why do certain ideas persist across centuries while others vanish?

The classical tradition matters to me not because it's old, but because it's good—and because engaging seriously with the past gives us better tools for thinking about the present.
